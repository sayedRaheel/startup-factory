### The Market Pain Point
Developers are increasingly adopting specialized AI agents for coding, QA, and release management (as seen with trending tools like `gstack` and `CLI-Anything`), but the orchestration layer remains heavily manual and fragmented. Meanwhile, project managers are acutely aware that their roles are prime targets for AI automation (evidenced by the anxiety in recent HN discussions). Currently, engineers have to manually bridge the gap between high-level project goals and the specific, iterative prompts needed to drive their local AI coding assistants. There is a glaring lack of a local-first, privacy-respecting orchestrator that can ingest raw requirements, translate them into actionable task graphs, and automatically manage the workflow of various specialized AI agents without relying on bloated, expensive SaaS platforms like Jira or Linear.

### The Proposed Solution
**LocalPM (or AgentFlow CLI):** An open-source, local-first AI Project Manager that lives entirely in your terminal. It directly integrates with your Git repository and local agent setups (like `Claude Code` or `OpenClaw`). You feed it a high-level `PRD.md` or a raw feature request, and it autonomously breaks the project down into granular sub-tasks, assigns them to specialized local agent personas (e.g., "Eng Manager", "QA Engineer"), coordinates their execution, resolves merge conflicts between agents, and automatically generates progress reports. It acts as the intelligent glue code between your project requirements and your raw CLI AI tools, creating a fully autonomous, local software development lifecycle.

### Why it will get GitHub Stars
"Fire Jira. Hire a Local AI Project Manager." Developers universally despise administrative overhead, status updates, and ticket management. By framing this tool as a terminal-native "Jira killer" that perfectly complements the massive trend of agentic dev tools (like `CLI-Anything`), it taps directly into the current hype cycle while solving a deeply rooted workflow annoyance. It promises developers the ultimate fantasy: the ability to focus purely on high-level architecture and vision while their local AI PM handles the tedious task breakdown, agent delegation, and progress tracking for free.
